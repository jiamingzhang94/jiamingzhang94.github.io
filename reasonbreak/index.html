ReasonBreak: Disrupting Hierarchical Reasoning

Adversarial Protection for Geographic Privacy in Multimodal Reasoning Models

Abstract

Multi-modal large reasoning models (MLRMs) pose significant privacy risks by inferring precise geographic locations from personal images through hierarchical chain-of-thought reasoning. Existing privacy protection techniques, primarily designed for perception-based models, prove ineffective against MLRMs' sophisticated multi-step reasoning processes that analyze environmental cues.

We introduce ReasonBreak, a novel adversarial framework specifically designed to disrupt hierarchical reasoning in MLRMs through concept-aware perturbations. Our approach is founded on the key insight that effective disruption of geographic reasoning requires perturbations aligned with conceptual hierarchies rather than uniform noise. ReasonBreak strategically targets critical conceptual dependencies within reasoning chains, generating perturbations that invalidate specific inference steps and cascade through subsequent reasoning stages.

To facilitate this approach, we contribute GeoPrivacy-6K, a comprehensive dataset comprising 6,341 ultra-high-resolution images with hierarchical concept annotations. Extensive evaluation across seven state-of-the-art MLRMs (including GPT-o3, GPT-5, Gemini 2.5 Pro) demonstrates ReasonBreak's superior effectiveness, significantly outperforming existing baselines.

Methodology

Our framework consists of three key stages: (1) Adaptive Image Decomposition to isolate localized geographic cues; (2) Minimax Target Selection to identify hard-negative priors that conceptually contradict the visual content; and (3) Concept-Aware Perturbation Generation using a learnable decoder.

GeoPrivacy-6K Dataset

To facilitate research in reasoning-aware privacy protection, we contribute GeoPrivacy-6K, a dataset comprising 6,341 ultra-high-resolution images. Each image is annotated with hierarchical geographic concepts (from continental to local levels) and precise bounding boxes, specifically designed to train models to disrupt visual reasoning chains.
